Book review - The Good Soldier Švejk

Author: Jaroslav Hašek

Language: Czech

— Podtarłeś sobie Arsch? — zapytał generał Szwejka.

— Posłusznie melduję, panie generale, że wszystko w porządku.

— Więcej srać nie będziesz?

— Posłusznie melduję, panie generale, że jestem fertig.

— No to zapnij spodnie jak się patrzy i stań na baczność.

Actually, I don't know which part of charm has been transmitted in the transation of the source text. If someday I'll start learning Czech language, "The Good Soldier Švejk" is going to be the first book I read.

But it wouldn't be enough. Jaroslav Hašek freely spoke Russian, German, English, French, Hungarian, Serbian, and even Bashkir. This story is about ordinary people, who used all the power of their language in everyday life. And so you can make strong phrases from many-many languages. E.g. (I can translate only Polish, so let's leave it as is):

  • Naši to zas někde v Srbsku prosrali (Czech)
  • Baszom az anyat, baszom az istenet, baszom a Kristus Mariat, baszom az atyadot, baszom a vilagot! (Hungarian)
  • Więcej srać nie będziesz? (Polish)

Anyway, I'm sure that everyone would get a lot of fun reading this book. It's a satiric story about ordinary retired soldier, a little bit idiotic (confirmed by doctors) and struggling to fight for the Emperor during World War I. He consequently follows a lot of locations and by the way meets many people who are involved in ongoing fight, showing them from different sides and unmasking an absurdity of everything is going on within this bloody conflict.

The best thing I like there is hundreds of funny stories about different people Švejk has for any situation. All of them start from something like One priest from Pisek... and can really make you laugh for hours.

In spite of its satiric essence, Švejk makes us think a lot about human nature. About the fact that behind all the global doings in the world you can always find a human being, who just wants to live, love, earn money and drink, and whatever else. It's definitely must read book for everyone.
